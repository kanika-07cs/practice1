Coimbatore is a historic city that is located in the tourist-friendly region of Tamil Nadu beside the Noyyal River. There are several possibilities available in Coimbatore if you want to shop, travel, and enjoy different South Indian traditional dishes. There are plenty of things to do for travelers, with numerous locations within 100 miles of Coimbatore. The best tourist sites are mentioned here to make it simple for visitors to organize their schedule while in Coimbatore.

Monkey falls

One of the top one-day trip destinations in Coimbatore, where you can enjoy the beautiful view of waterfalls. People traveling to this location need authorization from the forest department to visit these waterfalls. You can engage in trekking or even plan a picnic with your family, in addition to admiring the breathtaking beauty of the waterfalls. After 6 p.m. , the forest service forbids visitors from going to the waterfalls.

Pallakad Fort

Pallakad Fort, commonly known as the Tipu's Fort, was constructed by Hyder Ali in the 18th century. This fort is maintained by the Archaeological Survey of India and is visited by tourists all year round. You will adore this location if history is your area of interest.

Siruvani Fall

The Siruvani Fall, also known as Kovai Kutralam Falls, is created by the Siruvani river. With the money raised by the Tamil Nadu government, the Kerala government constructed the Siruvani reservoir for Tamil Nadu in order to satisfy the city of Coimbatore's drinking water needs. Being the second-sweetest lake water in the world, the water of the Dam is well known for its sweetness. It is a popular tourist destination in Coimbatore. Due to its location in the middle of a deep forest, visitors are not permitted to view the falls after 5:00 clock in the evening. The Siruvani dam is breathtakingly gorgeous and has a fantastic panoramic view.

Marudhamalai hill

The Marudhamalai hill temple is situated in the Western Ghats highland at a towering elevation of 500 feet. The temple is 15kilometers northwest of Coimbatore City and has a beautiful, energizing atmosphere. Vehicles are permitted to travel past the Marudhamalai temple. The devasthanam has authorized three buses to access the temple on top of the hill's lower slope. The principal deity of the Marudhamalai temple is Lord Muruga, and the temple ascribes pretty lovely amenities to the habit of making visitors' visits soothing.
